# Technician Interview Protocol
## Step 7 — 10 Contextual Interviews at 3 Dealerships

**Roadmap reference:** Phase 1, Deliverable 7
**Status:** `[~]` Protocol complete; interviews scheduled for Month 2
**Owner:** PM + UX Researcher
**Target:** 10 interviews across 3 dealerships; synthesis complete by Month 3

---

## Research Objectives

1. **Validate the pain point:** Confirm that complex EV repairs (battery TMS, hybrid inverter) are genuinely more painful than ICE repairs and that 2D tools are the root cause — not technician skill gaps.
2. **Map the actual workflow:** Understand what technicians *actually* do step-by-step, not what the manual says they should do.
3. **Identify interaction constraints:** What are technicians' hands doing during a repair? When can they look at a screen? What are the ambient conditions?
4. **Probe technology openness:** What is their prior XR experience? What do they expect would be annoying about a headset?
5. **Discover unknown pain:** What causes rework, warranty claims, or return visits that we haven't identified?

## Interview Structure (60 minutes)

### Part 1: Warm-Up (5 minutes)
Get technician comfortable; establish baseline.

- "How long have you been working here? What kinds of vehicles make up most of your day?"
- "Walk me through your last shift at a high level — what did you fix?"

---

### Part 2: A Specific Complex Repair (20 minutes)
The core of the contextual interview. Get a specific story, not generalities.

**Opening:** "I want to hear about a specific recent repair that was harder than it should have been. Not a simple oil change — something where you had to really dig in. What comes to mind?"

**Follow-up probes (use as needed, not as a checklist):**
1. "Take me back to the beginning — when did you first see the vehicle? What was on the repair order?"
2. "What did you do first to figure out what was wrong?"
3. "What tools did you use? Show me on this diagram where you were standing and what you were looking at."
4. "Were there moments where you weren't sure what to do next? Tell me about one."
5. "Did you have to look anything up? What did you look at — a screen, a paper manual, something else?"
6. "How often did you have to stop touching the car and look at something? What was annoying about that?"
7. "How long did the diagnosis take? How long did it take to actually fix it once you knew what was wrong?"
8. "Did the car come back? Did the repair stick the first time?"
9. "If you could change one thing about that experience, what would it be?"

**Observation prompt (if in bay):** "Can you show me where the tool or screen would have been while you were working?"

---

### Part 3: EV-Specific Probes (15 minutes)
If the story from Part 2 wasn't EV-related, pivot here.

10. "How is diagnosing an EV or hybrid different from a regular gas car? What's harder?"
11. "Have you had to deal with a battery thermal management issue? What was that like?"
12. "When you're working near high-voltage systems, what goes through your mind? What are you careful about?"
13. "Has anyone ever gotten hurt or had a close call with HV in your shop or one you've heard of?"
14. "Do you feel like your training fully prepared you for EV repairs, or are there gaps?"

---

### Part 4: Technology & Openness (15 minutes)
Probe current tool satisfaction and XR openness *without* showing the product.

15. "What's your favorite tool for diagnosis right now? What makes it good?"
16. "What's the most annoying tool or process you use regularly? Why do you keep using it?"
17. "Have you ever used augmented reality or a VR headset? Where?"
18. "If someone gave you a pair of smart glasses that could show you a 3D diagram of what's inside the engine while you worked — what's the first thing you'd want to see?"
19. "What would make you NOT want to use something like that?"
20. "Your hands are usually busy when you work — how do you deal with needing to check a screen or a phone?"

---

### Part 5: Wrap-Up (5 minutes)
21. "If you could wave a magic wand and improve one thing about your day-to-day work — not just diagnostics, anything — what would it be?"
22. "Is there anything you think I should know that I didn't ask about?"
23. Confirm: can we follow up by email if we have more questions?

## Interview Logistics

| Detail | Plan |
|---|---|
| Format | In-person, in the service bay (contextual) — not a conference room |
| Recording | Audio recording with consent; notes taken on tablet |
| Observation | Ask to observe 1 active repair if time permits after interview (15–30 min add-on) |
| Facilitation | PM leads; UX researcher takes notes; no product team members present (biases responses) |
| Consent form | Signed before interview; covers audio recording and use of anonymized quotes |

## Analysis Framework

After all 10 interviews, synthesize findings using:

**Jobs to Be Done (JTBD):**
- What job is the technician hiring a diagnostic tool to do?
- What are the functional, emotional, and social dimensions of that job?

**Affinity Mapping:**
- Group observations by theme: workflow friction, attention switching, confidence/uncertainty, safety concerns, training gaps
- Identify which themes appear in ≥7 of 10 interviews (high confidence findings)
- Identify themes unique to EV vs. ICE repairs

**Persona Validation:**
- Does "Alex Rivera" hold up? What needs updating?
- Are there sub-personas (apprentice vs. master tech) with meaningfully different needs?

**Key Deliverable:** 1-page research synthesis document with top 5 validated findings and 3 invalidated assumptions, delivered to full team within 1 week of last interview.
